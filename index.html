Method, in brief

Both uses of biomass are put on a common metric: dollars per tonne of CO₂ climate benefit. For SAF the benefit is fossil jet fuel displaced; for CDR it is CO₂ permanently removed; FT-SAF + CCS earns both.

Cost formulas

SAF: (MFSP × gallons − jet fuel revenue − 45Z credit − 45Q on stored CO₂) ÷ tCO₂ benefit. MFSP (minimum fuel selling price) is feedstock-inclusive. Gallons per dry ton follow from mass yield (kg fuel / kg feedstock ÷ 0.8 kg/L ÷ 3.79 L/gal); each gallon displaces 9.75 kg CO₂ of fossil jet (EIA). FT-SAF + CCS is not priced independently: its MFSP is the FT-SAF (ag/forest) cost plus the CCS cost slider applied to the ~0.018 tCO₂ stored per gallon (54% of biomass carbon exits as a concentrated CO₂ stream during gasification, so capture is cheap; the cost is mostly transport and storage).

CDR: (total cost − co-product revenue − 45Q) ÷ tCO₂ removed. Costs are feedstock-inclusive and calibrated to actual offtake pricing. A dry ton of biomass holds 1.83 tCO₂e; removal = 1.83 × capture efficiency.

Subsidies

The 45Q credit ($85/tCO₂ statutory) requires gaseous CO₂ capture — BECCS, WtE+CCS, and FT-SAF+CCS qualify; bio-oil, biochar, burial, and injection do not. The 45Z Clean Fuel Production Credit (post-OBBBA: up to $1.00/gal with prevailing-wage compliance, through 2029) is scaled by carbon intensity, (50 − CI)/50 — at typical SAF lifecycle reductions it is worth ~$0.35–0.45/gal, and it varies with each Monte Carlo draw.

The 45V clean hydrogen production credit survived OBBBA but was shortened: facilities must begin construction before January 1, 2028 (previously 2033) to claim up to $3.00/kg for ten years. 45V and 45Q cannot be claimed at the same facility (§45V(d)(2)), so the BECCS (hydrogen) pathway carries an election toggle — 45Q on stored CO₂ (default) or 45V on the hydrogen, worth ~$126/tCO₂ removed at the default 65% capture efficiency. Deeply carbon-negative biomass-gasification hydrogen readily meets the 0.45 kgCO₂e/kg threshold for the full rate. Its co-product revenue default ($130/tCO₂ ≈ $3.1/kg H₂) is a market price between gray and green hydrogen, independent of either credit.

Accounting toggles

Simple displacement credits SAF with the full combustion emissions of the jet fuel it replaces. Full lifecycle multiplies SAF's benefit by its lifecycle GHG reduction (CORSIA-style, typically 50–85%), applies 95% net-negativity to CDR storage, and credits CDR co-products (electricity, heat, hydrogen) with the fossil emissions they displace.

Caveats

Avoided ≠ removed: SAF benefits are counterfactual and shrink if aviation decarbonizes another way; CDR removals are permanent. Non-CO₂ aviation effects (contrails) are not modeled — crediting them could roughly double SAF's benefit. No commercial FT-SAF+CCS plant exists; those costs are techno-economic estimates. HEFA uses waste fats and oils, which mostly do not compete with cellulosic CDR for the same feedstock and are supply-limited (~5–10 Mt/yr globally).
